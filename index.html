    Synopsis

    The incidents around Donald Trump's curated meme-coin have left the entire crypto community flabbergasted, with many claiming that this is one big bluff that the new US President may have pulled on US crypto investors.

    Donald Trump was supported by a majority of crypto shareholders as they expected that once he joined office, the new US President would begin taking necessary steps to legitimize the currency and ensure that regulations around crypto in the US become a little more lenient than what it was during the Biden administration.

    Crypto fans infuriated with Trump over meme-coins

    However, what Trump did next was absolutely preposterous according to some top crypto investors. The new US President, just as he was to become US President, announced that his own meme-coin was here, and crypto investors could begin purchasing it.

    In the very initial stage, Trump was offering a whopping 20 million meme-coins at very nominal prices, which could be bought and traded on the Solana blockchain. This infuriated crypto investors as they expected that Trump would have worked to improve the crypto infrastructure, and not go all solo, selling his own set of crypto meme-coins.

    Melania Trump's meme-coin became the biggest heartbreak

    What was worse came next, as his wife, Melania Trump launched her own exclusive meme-coin, which could again be traded or bought on the Solana blockchain. This was the nail in the coffin for crypto enthusiasts in the US, who are now saying that the presidential family is 'grifting at the expense of the entire crypto fraternity in the United States', according to a Slate report.
